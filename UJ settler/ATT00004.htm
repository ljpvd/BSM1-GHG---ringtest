Hi,

note that it is not necessarily correct that the average concentrations for solubles are the same in reac 5, settler effl and settler underflow. This is the results that WEST show all the time. To get that result your diurnal dynamics in the input would have to identical day after day after day. Do not know if your input test file is constructed like that. If not,  there is an error with WEST , or they are using modeltype=2 i.e. settler output soluble concentrations identical to settler input concentrations with no delays, whereas you try to use modeltype =0 in matlab i.e. ten layers with complete mix for the solubles.

The main concern was this: Your DO increases in the settler compared to reactor 5. Not correct, it must be (almost) identical to reactor 5 average over the full day if you have simulated if for some time before the evaluation day under similar conditions. Also your SNO2 SN2 and SN2O are very different in the settler underflow and overflow compared to reactor 5, just as if you where still using a reactive settler of some kind.

But you can get such results if your input dynamics are quite different the day prior to the evaluation day sonce what you get during your 24 hours of evaluation time will depend on the system status prior to that time. But as I said before I do not know how your input test data is constructed.

/Ulf
